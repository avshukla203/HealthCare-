👶 Mother & Child Care

Complete guide to postpartum recovery and newborn wellness

💪 Physical Healing & Body Care

Rest: The most crucial element. Limit visitors and try to sleep when the baby sleeps to combat exhaustion.

Perineal Care: For vaginal deliveries, use a peri bottle with warm water to clean the area, take soothing sitz baths, and use ice packs to reduce swelling.

C-Section Care: Keep the incision site clean and dry. Avoid heavy lifting and consult your doctor if you notice redness, swelling, or severe pain.

Hydration & Nutrition: Drink at least 8-10 glasses of water daily, especially if breastfeeding. Focus on nutrient-rich foods (iron, protein, calcium) to regain strength.

Pelvic Floor: Do gentle exercises like walking and pelvic floor contractions (Kegels), but always clear strenuous routines with your healthcare provider first.

🍼 Breastfeeding & Breast Care

Comfort & Latching: Ensure your baby has a deep latch to prevent nipple soreness. Use lanolin cream or nipple shields if necessary.

Relief: Use warm compresses before nursing to help with let-down, and cold compresses or gel pads afterward to reduce engorgement.

🧠 Mental & Emotional Well-being

Baby Blues vs. PPD: It is common to experience the "baby blues" (mood swings, anxiety) for the first few weeks. However, if feelings of sadness, anxiety, or detachment last longer or worsen, seek immediate support from a healthcare professional.

⚠️ Warning Signs — When to Call a Doctor

Reach out to your gynecologist or healthcare provider immediately if you experience any of the following:

• Fever
• Heavy vaginal bleeding (soaking more than one pad per hour)
• Severe abdominal or pelvic pain
• Foul-smelling vaginal discharge
• Signs of mastitis (red, painful, or warm areas on the breast, along with flu-like symptoms)
